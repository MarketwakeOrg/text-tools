Free Character Counter and Title Case Converter

Marketwake Text Tools is a free online character counter and title case converter built for marketers, writers, and content creators who need fast, accurate text analysis. Paste your copy into the editor and instantly see your character count, word count, sentence count, paragraph count, and more. Need to check keyword density for a blog post or landing page? Type your target keyword and get real-time occurrence and density data. No signups, no clutter, just the information you need to write better content.

The title case converter helps you format headings and titles to match industry-standard style guides. Whether you follow APA, Chicago Manual of Style, AP, MLA, or New York Times conventions, select your preferred guide and your text is converted automatically. You can also switch between sentence case, uppercase, lowercase, and alternating case with a single click. It is the fastest way to clean up headlines, email subject lines, ad copy, and social media posts before they go live.

Frequently Asked Questions

This tool counts characters (with and without spaces), words, sentences, paragraphs, and lines in real time as you type or paste text. It also calculates reading time, speaking time, average word length, and letter frequency so you get a complete picture of your content at a glance.
Type your target keyword or phrase into the keyword density field and the tool scans your text for exact matches. It returns the total number of occurrences, the keyword density as a percentage of total words, and the overall word count. This is helpful for SEO content writing where you want to hit a target keyword density without over-optimizing.
The title case converter supports five major style guides: APA (7th edition), Chicago Manual of Style, Associated Press (AP), MLA, and New York Times. Each guide has slightly different rules about which words to capitalize, especially articles, prepositions, and conjunctions. Select your guide and the converter handles the rest.
Beyond title case, you can convert text to sentence case, UPPERCASE, lowercase, alternating case, first letter capitalization (every word), and inverse case. These options are useful for formatting headlines, social media posts, email subject lines, and any other content that needs consistent capitalization.

Character limits are everywhere in digital marketing. Google meta titles display roughly 50 to 60 characters. Meta descriptions cut off around 155 to 160 characters. Google Ads headlines allow 30 characters, descriptions allow 90. Social platforms like X (Twitter), LinkedIn, and Facebook each have their own limits for posts and bios. Staying within these limits ensures your copy displays correctly and communicates your message without getting truncated.
